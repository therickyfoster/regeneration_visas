Primary Immigration Pathways

The Restoration Visa can be implemented through multiple existing immigration pathways. Each has distinct processing times, requirements, and retention characteristics.

Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)

Processing: 6-18 months (varies by province)

Annual Allocation: 55,000 spaces (2025-2027)

CRS Bonus: 600 points (virtually guarantees ITA)

Retention: 85-94% in rural programs

‚úì Recommended primary pathway - provincial control, employer-driven, proven retention

Express Entry Category-Based

Processing: 6 months average

CRS Cutoff: Typically 470-500 for categories

Annual Invitations: Unlimited within overall targets

Retention: 60-70% typical for EE

‚úì Fast processing but lower rural retention without settlement support

Rural Community Immigration Pilot

Processing: 12-15 months

Communities: 14 participating (Brandon, Trail, etc.)

Endorsement Required: Community recommendation

Retention: 87% (predecessor RNIP)

‚úì Strongest rural retention - community integration model

Atlantic Immigration Model

Processing: 6-12 months

Provinces: NB, NS, PE, NL

Settlement Services: Included for temp workers

Retention: 94% first-year

‚úì Proven model - adapt for restoration sector nationally

Typical Processing Timeline (PNP Stream)

Month 0-1: Provincial Nomination Application

Applicant submits to provincial program. Requires job offer from designated restoration employer, credential assessment, language test results, and demonstration of restoration experience/education.

Month 2-4: Provincial Assessment

Province reviews application against stream criteria. May request additional documentation. Strong applications with employer support typically approved within 2-3 months.

Month 4-5: Nomination Certificate Issued

Upon approval, province issues nomination certificate. Applicant receives 600 CRS points in Express Entry pool, virtually guaranteeing Invitation to Apply (ITA) in next draw.

Month 5-6: Express Entry ITA

Applicant receives ITA from IRCC. Must submit complete permanent residence application within 60 days. Includes medical exam, police certificates, proof of funds.

Month 6-12: Federal Processing

IRCC processes PR application. PNP applications receive priority processing (6-month service standard for complete applications). Background checks, document verification.

Month 12-14: Permanent Residence Granted

Applicant receives Confirmation of Permanent Residence (COPR). Must land in Canada within validity period (typically 1 year). Upon landing, becomes permanent resident with full settlement service access.

Expedited Processing Opportunity: IRCC's Digital Platform Modernization ($827.3M over 5 years) aims to reduce processing times. Restoration Visa could be designated priority stream, targeting 4-6 month federal processing similar to Express Entry service standards.

Provincial Stream Design Template

Provinces can configure their restoration stream using these parameters. All must align with federal IRCC requirements while allowing provincial customization.

Mandatory Federal Requirements

Valid job offer from designated restoration employer (minimum 1 year full-time)
Educational credential assessment showing equivalency to Canadian standards
Language test results meeting minimum CLB thresholds for NOC
Sufficient settlement funds (unless working in Canada on valid permit)
Admissibility (no criminal inadmissibility, medical inadmissibility)
Intent to reside in nominating province

Provincial Customization Options

Specific restoration domain priorities (e.g., wildfire recovery for BC, watershed for MB)
Regional distribution requirements (rural vs urban placement)
Indigenous partnership requirements (FPIC compliance, Guardian program integration)
Carbon credit revenue-sharing mandates
Monitoring and reporting protocols (SER standards, remote sensing verification)
Settlement service commitments from employers
Points-based ranking system within stream (if application volume exceeds allocation)

All immigration pathways must include robust worker protections: (1) Labor standards enforcement preventing exploitation, (2) Transparent wage disclosure requirements, (3) Rights education provided in applicant's language, (4) Independent complaint mechanisms without immigration consequences for workers reporting violations, (5) Employer sanctions for non-compliance including designation removal, (6) Settlement service access from arrival regardless of immigration status.
