UNDRIP & Bill C-15: Foundational Requirements

The United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (UNDRIP), implemented through Bill C-15 (2021), provides the legal framework for Indigenous partnership. The 2023 UNDRIP Action Plan contains 181 specific measures requiring Indigenous engagement on resource projects.

Article 19: Free, Prior and Informed Consent (FPIC)

States shall consult and cooperate in good faith with the Indigenous peoples concerned through their own representative institutions in order to obtain their free, prior and informed consent before adopting and implementing legislative or administrative measures that may affect them.

Application to Restoration Visa: Any restoration work on Indigenous territories requires nation-to-nation consultation before visa allocation. Indigenous nations have the right to say no.

Article 26: Rights to Lands, Territories & Resources

Indigenous peoples have the right to the lands, territories and resources which they have traditionally owned, occupied or otherwise used or acquired. States shall give legal recognition and protection to these lands, territories and resources.

Application to Restoration Visa: Land stewardship decisions rest with Indigenous nations. Restoration Visa must support Indigenous-led conservation rather than imposing external priorities.

Article 32: Development Affecting Lands

States shall consult and cooperate in good faith with the Indigenous peoples concerned through their own representative institutions in order to obtain their free and informed consent prior to the approval of any project affecting their lands or territories and other resources.

Application to Restoration Visa: Restoration projects are development activities requiring consent. Visa workers cannot be deployed to Indigenous territories without nation approval and participation in governance.

First Nations National Guardians Network

Launched at COP15 (December 2022) as the world's first Indigenous-led national stewardship network. Now independently manages federal funding allocation, reducing agreement processing from 6 months to 60 days for 90% of cases.

Restoration Visa Integration Model:

Partner with the National Guardians Network for program governance. Allocate minimum 25% of visa placements to Indigenous-led restoration initiatives. Provide training pathways for Guardian programs to employ visa holders under Indigenous supervision. Ensure revenue-sharing from restoration outcomes (carbon credits, ecosystem services valuation) flows to Indigenous communities.

Indigenous Data Sovereignty: OCAP¬Æ Principles

The First Nations Information Governance Centre has trademarked OCAP¬Æ to prevent misuse. Any data collection related to Indigenous peoples or territories must follow these principles:

Ownership

Indigenous communities own information about their members, lands, and resources. Data belongs to the community that it's about.

Control

Indigenous peoples control all aspects of research and data management processes. This includes collection methods, access, analysis, and disclosure.

Access

Indigenous peoples have the right to access data about themselves and their communities regardless of where it's held.

Possession

While ownership resides with the community, possession refers to stewardship. Physical control of data may be held by Indigenous organizations.

Restoration Visa Application:

Any monitoring data from restoration projects on Indigenous territories must be collected with community consent. Data hosting should preferably occur on Indigenous-controlled servers. Analysis and publication require community approval. Carbon credit revenues derived from data belong to Indigenous communities.
